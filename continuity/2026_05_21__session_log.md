## Diagnosis of record — three bugs

### Bug 1 — C_house recovery (optimizer coordinate conditioning)

The §6 Stage 3 fit recovered C_house at 4.893e6 against truth 4.5e6 (~8.7%
high), bit-identical across every run, on telemetry generated by its own
forward chain at C_house = 4.5e6.

Five hypotheses were falsified by direct probe, in order:

1. **Synthesizer distribution-handling (`eta_distribution`)** — eliminated
   2026-05-20; the 0.90→1.0 change moved the C_house posterior by nothing.
2. **D17 total-vs-sensible capacity channel / SHR** — falsified 2026-05-21.
   A physically-correct latent split, derived per-sample from F5's D19 SHR
   bi-quadratic (replacing the hand-set `moisture_removal_kg_per_s = 1.0e-4`),
   moved C_house by nothing. This overturned the 2026-05-20 Map open
   question 1 and its "live moisture-rate finding."
3. **Prior pull** — the base prior (C_house 5.0e6 ± 7.5e5) is too weak
   against a likelihood this steep to explain the offset.
4. **Welch band-power objective as a biased estimator** — falsified. The
   Stage 3 objective, swept directly with co-parameters at truth, minimizes
   at exactly 4.5e6 (negloglik 2.5e-5 at truth, climbing steeply either side).
5. **Co-parameter error absorption from the §5 passive fit** — falsified.
   The objective minimizes C_house at 4.5e6 with the six co-parameters held
   at either truth or the §5 passive posterior.

**Actual cause: optimizer coordinate conditioning in `_laplace_with_objective`
(`staged_fit.py`).** The seven canonical parameters span roughly seven orders
of magnitude (~1 to ~5e6). L-BFGS-B applies one step length and one
inverse-Hessian scaling across all seven coordinates at once, so C_house, the
largest, is effectively frozen — its gradient is correct (measured ~+4.3e-5
per unit C_house, consistent across finite-difference steps from 1e-8 to 1e3)
but unusable at a step size shared with order-1 parameters. The optimizer
reports `success=True` having never moved C_house from its start. Not a
finite-difference resolution problem — a step-length/conditioning problem.

**Confirmation:** the identical optimizer, on the identical objective, run in
normalized coordinates (each parameter divided by its scale) descended
C_house from the prior 5.0e6 to 4.520e6 — within 0.4% of truth.

**Fix:** `_laplace_with_objective` runs L-BFGS-B in normalized coordinates
`u = θ / scale`, with `scale` = the prior's marginal sigmas; bounds and
multi-restart starts are mapped into u-space and `result.x` mapped back. The
finite-difference Hessian step was deliberately left in raw coordinates
(flagged by a code comment) — the §6 rerun's CI coverage confirmed the
Hessian did not need the same treatment. Verified: Stage 3 C_house recovers
to truth, test green.

### Bug 2 — C_w recovery (§11.2 specification defect)

Once the C_house fix unfroze the Stage 4 fit, C_w recovered at ~5.4 against
truth 45 under the then-current units. Not a code bug — a specification
defect, faithfully implemented.

The §11.2 amendment named C_w as the "whole-house lumped latent moisture
capacitance" but routed it to `run_dynamic`'s `kappa_buffer_override` — the
κ_buffer *coefficient* slot (physical value ~0.05) — with no conversion,
unlike the C_house entry, which specifies its SI→imperial conversion.
`real_chain.py` faithfully did `kappa_buffer = c_w` raw, with its own comment
flagging "units convention not yet tight." A whole-house capacitance routed
into a per-area-coefficient slot is a category error.

**Decision (JDS, Option B):** C_w *is* the whole-house capacitance, SI units
(kg); `real_chain` converts to the κ_buffer coefficient by inverting
`capacitance.py`'s affine map `C_w = m_air + κ_buffer · A_interior`. The
adapter recovers the map by probing the public `house_moisture_capacity` at
two κ values — no private-constant coupling — parallel to the C_house path.

**Prior re-derived from the building physics:** known air-mass offset
(~857 kg) plus a buffering term spanning the κ_buffer literature range. Base
prior mean **2400 kg**, σ **1000 kg**; test truth **2100 kg** (−0.3σ);
`positive_floors["C_w"]` raised **1.0 → 1000 kg** (C_w cannot fall below the
bare air mass). Fix applied across four files — `real_chain.py` (conversion),
`staged_fit.py` (floor), `passive_fit_types.py` (`make_acca_manual_j_fallback_prior`
mean/σ), `test_g8_closed_loop.py` (`THETA_TRUE_PERTURBED`) — and the §11.2
amendment corrected to specify the conversion. Verified: Stage 4 C_w
recovers, test green.

### Bug 3 — frozen-SHR over-drying (live-evaluation HVAC provider)

The C_w fix made the integrated moisture capacitance physically correct
(~4,600 lb-equivalent, against the old ~422,000), so moisture dynamics run
~90× faster. That exposed a latent defect in the §6 measured-HVAC
synthesizer: it computed indoor wet-bulb once at initial conditions and used
it frozen for both the delivered-capacity evaluation and the D19 SHR split.
With a frozen SHR, latent removal does not taper as the air dries, so `W_in`
was driven slightly negative and rejected by `DynamicState`. Confirmed not a
numerical-integration artifact — the continuous moisture solution itself
crosses zero; RK4 would not fix it.

**Decision (JDS):** the §6 measured-HVAC excitation becomes a live evaluation
of the equipment characterization. Tracing the data flow clarified the
design: only the **synthesizer** live-evaluates — it runs the forward chain
with a state-aware provider that evaluates `day4_posterior` (capacity) and
the D19 SHR against the integrator's evolving indoor wet-bulb, generating a
physically-consistent excitation trace that tapers as the house dries. The
**fit consumes that recorded trace unchanged**. This matches the real-pilot
model (the fit uses measured HVAC telemetry) and dissolves the
real-pilot-reconciliation concern rather than adding it as debt.

**Fix** across three files — `forward_chain.py` (`StateTrajectory` gains
optional applied-HVAC fields), `real_chain.py` (a `make_live_hvac_provider`
builder, a wet-bulb helper, an optional `hvac_provider` argument on `run`,
and applied-HVAC extraction), and the §6 test synthesizer (builds the live
provider, runs the chain, records the applied trace as the window's
excitation). Verified: §6 closed-loop passes 3/3; `W_in` stays positive
through Phase A with no integrator-boundary guard needed.

## Probe trail

Each probe was a short scratch script run against the real chain; each
falsified one hypothesis or confirmed one fact. Scratch files were written to
the tests directory and swept with the Temp Folder discipline; none were
committed.

- `probe_prior.py` — printed the base prior. C_house 5.0e6 ± 7.5e5; truth
  4.5e6 is a deliberate ~−8% perturbation, matching the perturbation pattern
  of the other six `THETA_TRUE_PERTURBED` entries.
- `probe_welch.py` — swept the Stage 3 objective vs C_house with co-parameters
  at truth. Minimum at exactly 4.5e6. Also confirmed the Stage 3 Welch
  geometry: the 3 h window caps the resolvable period at 3 h, so the in-band
  content is six bins (periods 0.5 h to 3 h) — thin but not biased.
- `probe_compensation.py` — re-swept with co-parameters at the §5 passive
  posterior instead of truth. Minimum still at 4.5e6. Co-parameter error
  absorption eliminated.
- `probe_optimizer.py` — instrumented the Stage 3 prior and ran L-BFGS-B from
  the prior mean. Bounds contain truth; the objective grid confirms the bowl;
  L-BFGS-B returns C_house at exactly 5.0e6 (zero movement), `success=True`.
- `probe_scaling.py` — measured the C_house gradient (correct, ~+4.3e-5,
  across all FD steps) and ran L-BFGS-B unscaled vs normalized. Unscaled
  5.0e6; normalized 4.520e6. Confirmed the fix before a line of `staged_fit.py`
  was changed.

The C_w and SHR bugs were diagnosed by code-trace and test-failure analysis
rather than by probe — the C_w defect was found by reading the §11.2
amendment against `capacitance.py` and `real_chain.py`; the frozen-SHR defect
was found by tracing the `DynamicState` rejection back to the synthesizer's
once-computed wet-bulb.

## Committed workstream — analytic-truth forward verification battery

Owner decision (JDS, 2026-05-21). Extends the existing D1–D5 discipline.
Motivation: closed-loop recovery tests share the forward chain between truth
and fit, so a defect *in the forward physics* passes green on both sides —
the structural blind spot that let all three of today's bugs through across
multiple sessions. The battery verifies the forward model against
hand-computable answers on deliberately trivial inputs: a sensible-only case
(closed-form steady-state ΔT and τ = R·C), a latent-only case (closed-form
moisture balance, W_in stays positive), a coupled HVAC-on case (delivered
capacity, SHR split, and steady-state (T,W) all matching a hand calculation),
and a conservation check. Built against the now-corrected forward model — the
live-evaluation provider it sequences after is finished — then guards every
change after that. Recorded as workstream T13 in the Critical Path Map.
